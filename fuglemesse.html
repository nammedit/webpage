Fuglemesse

This is in some ways a very personal piece for me. Commissioned by birdwatcher Vibeke Steinsholm on behalf of Vega Verdensarvsenter I was asked to make a "mass for the homecoming of birds" for recitor, electronics and a group of 5-8 children. The birds and area in question is my home-disctrict in Norway called "Sør-Helgeland", and I were to use sounds from the birds I grew up hearing on the island of Vega where my father is from and its surrounding ocean of smaller islands.

As a way of starting off the project, I was invited to visit a small group of islands outside Vega called "Hysværet" and stay there for some days to experience the environment, listen to and record birdsong. I quickly became interested in the idea creating a pseudo-mythology for the islands which included a creation-myth explaning birds and their spiritual role in this island-world. I didn't end up creating an elaborate myth with texts about creation and so on, but these ideas and images of birds having a spiritual role stayed with me and became very important when I started composing with their sounds in the electronics and the material for the children's choir.

Then after having struggled for some months to create or find the appropriate text for the piece myself, me and Vibeke agreed upon contacting poet Rawdna Carita Eira to write the text for the piece in both Northern-Norwegian and the almost extinct language of South-Sami. From here on the compositional process could really start.
